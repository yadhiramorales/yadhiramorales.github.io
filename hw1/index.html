What are "The Big Five"?

General Overview

The “Big Five” refers to the top five tech companies in the United States: Amazon, Alphabet (Google), Apple, Meta (Facebook), and Microsoft. This group goes by several acronyms, such as GAMAM, GAFAM, and FAAMG. These companies are among the most influential globally, just behind NVIDIA, which currently stands as the world’s largest company. Combined, the Big Five hold a market capitalization of more than $14.5 trillion as of mid-September 2025.

The companies and their approximate market caps are:

  • Microsoft: $3.8 trillion
  • Apple: $3.5 trillion
  • Alphabet (Google): $2.9 trillion
  • Amazon: $2.9 trillion
  • Meta (Facebook): $2.4 trillion

The Big Five have amassed such high value not only due to their massive market value and user bases, but also for the extensive range of sectors these companies are involved in, from healthcare to social media to digital infrastructure.

Key Facts

  • These tech companies are leaders in innovation, creating new technologies and constatntly expanding into new markets.
  • They shape the digital economy, how people access services, communicate, consume media, and much more.
  • They not only grow through new technology breakthroughs, but also through expanding via aquisitions, and reinforcing exisitng tools or fields.
